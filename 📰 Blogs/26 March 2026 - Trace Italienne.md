The walls are rapidly closing in on the digital world, and most developers, left atomised, will undoubtedly be put through the squeeze as they begin to attempt developing everything themselves. Social media age verification, bans, the increasing prevalence of LLM traffic and supply chain attacks, the SaaS-ification of nearly every service, even those that have a fixed development cost, and the equivalent raising of rates. 

Worse, it cuts off the substrata of users we interact with daily on platforms not under our control. **The only solution is autarky, combined with elastic defence**.

# Race Against The Clock

Most of us have pressing real-world responsibilities and the enthusiastic programmers we know have skills ill-equipped to solve the problem. The experienced programmers we know have jobs for which they are paid, and are now contributing to the problem. 

Our assessment is that sometime by late 2027-2028, the internet will be rendered uninhabitable from a Western perspective, and the experience of its usage will be somewhat similar to those living in China or Russia in terms of mandatory identification, surveillance, legal powers, creeping subscription costs, LLM/botted traffic ('bad bots') attempting to pose as social media users, and everyone's favourite agentic workflow of supply chain attacks on GitHub and marketplace extensions. The future is Chinese, indeed. 

Institutions in the West have rapidly been captured by a 'nobody but us' mindset and thoroughly propagandised. If you are not prepared for total information warfare, you are not prepared for tomorrow. Achieving some form of digital sovereignty therefore has to be the top priority for anyone in the field, and yet no one has the time to do it. People can only see 8 hours in front of them. To this end, by the end of 2026, we must have:

**Core Infrastructure**
*Round brackets to the right of a description denote the Confoederatio software currently plugging this gap*.

1. Communications: (End 2026)
	1. A social media/centralised communications dashboard which can interface with other platforms similarly to a userbot with a single GUI. Since userbases cannot be ported, this ensures redundancy. None.
2. Fundamental office software and development imperatives: (Near-complete)
	1. A development IDE. (Scriptly IDE/Vercengen)
	2. Mapping software that also acts as an OSINT/geospatial news portal to remove reliance on mainstream media (Collation/Naissance HGIS)
	3. Office suite software: spreadsheets (Vercengen), word processors (Vercengen), browsers (Blacktraffic/Vercengen), though note in the latter case that much more work is neeeded in ensuring an automated and secure browsing experience.
3. Ground-truth datasets: (June 2026)
	1. Histmap - economic, demographic, urban, and political historical data, structured and digitised so as to be useful (SVEA).
	2. Livemap - a real-time digital twin of the world. Likely, archived 3D tile data is also necessary at some point in the future (Collation).
	3. Digital archives and mirrors of Archive/David Rumsey/Sci-Hub/Libgen, and other sources deemed at risk of failure or link rot. This likely requires some level of upfront infrastructure (Preservés).
4. Local agents. Necessary for scraping and filtering, as well as for NLP tasks in an age of total information warfare. None.
# Conclusion

This sort of hardware can be expensive and difficult to justify to non-developers, of course, and some convincing is in order. Unfortunately, the top-down tragedy of the commons from politicians, journos, and corpos has forced us into a race against the clock. SVEA is naturally set to complete at the end of June 2026, after which we will immediately pivot to ensuring social/comms redundancy.
