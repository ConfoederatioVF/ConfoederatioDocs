There are now 7-8 days left on the clock before my software engine, [[Vercengen]], is due for its **1.0 Release**. There is little point in pushing for other people to adopt it however - production is cheaper than effective talk. 

When you work with a computer, it is centred on you. When you 'work' with people, they are (without exception) centred on themselves. The only way one can convince someone to actually use your software is if you reach them on software that they use, i.e. by modding, introducing a videogame into their life that they play, or creating UGC (user-generated content) on platforms that they use at a routine pace that can be passively consumed. If you are asking them to talk to you (i.e. on Discord or Reddit), that is too much effort for people to consider.

This makes producing software incredibly cheap, but its adoption incredibly expensive. Promoting its adoption would effectively be a full-time endeavour you'd have to content mill 20 hours a day for, as it requires you to saturate your UGC across extant social media platforms at a steady clip, meaning that it would forestall progress on your own software work entirely.

And even if you are able to coax a single beta tester or contributor to hop onboard your project, it is likely that their retention will not be for long. Online personas are infamously fickle, and you can expect that they will be gone within the average lifespan of a fruit fly. 

## Parallel Systems

Whenever you want to talk to someone on social media, you must be the initiator of the discussion, not other people, since other people are basically human stand-ins for ChatGPT (they must always be prompted first without exception), hallucinate perhaps just as often, and can be reached by instant message only once every several business months. Of course you can't even ask them for documentation or to perform data entry, as they are only interested in arguments and niche consumerist interests.

The fact that they argue so much makes them an incredibly effective target to rubberduck on or bounce ideas off of, or come up with new talking points and soundbites, but that is all that other online humans are good for.

Given that relying on other people is a joke if you wish to get anything done, it is best to leverage and work with computers, not people. Build your own parallel system. Detach from digital society. It is not necessary.
